# Work Glossary

> Terms, acronyms, and definitions picked up from client projects and the day-to-day.

## B

**Barrel Cam** - a mechanical device, typically a cylinder, with a machined groove, ridge, or slot on its surface that converts rotational motion into precise linear motion, usually parallel to the cylinder's axis. ==Also known as a cylindrical cam== #hairtransplanttool

**Biocompatibility** - the ability of a material or medical device to perform its intended function with an appropriate host response, without eliciting adverse local or systemic effects #QMS 

---

## D

**DOE** — Design of Experiments
- A structured method for determining the relationship between factors affecting a process and the output. Common in engineering and product testing. #QMS

**Dermis** — the layer of skin beneath the epidermis, containing connective tissue, hair follicles, and sweat glands. #hairtransplanttool 

---

## E

**EMI** — Electromagnetic Interference
Disturbance generated by an external source that affects an electrical circuit. Relevant to wearable and medical devices.
- Can cause pacemakers or ICDs to malfunction (unnecessary shocks or failure to deliver necessary ones)
- Symptoms in patients: lightheadedness, dizziness, palpitations

**Epidermis** — the surface epithelium of the skin, overlying the dermis. #hairtransplanttool 

---

## F

**FUE** — Follicular Unit Extraction
A method of hair transplant surgery where individual follicular units are extracted directly from the scalp. #hairtransplanttool 

**FUI** - Follicular Unit Inserstion #hairtransplanttool 

**Follicular Unit** — a naturally occurring grouping of 1–4 (sometimes up to 6) hair follicles, along with sebaceous (oil) glands, nerves, blood vessels, and a small muscle (arrector pili). #hairtransplanttool 

---

## G

**Gearhead** - a mechanical device, often integrated with a motor (gear motor), designed to reduce output speed and increase torque. ==Also known as a gearbox==
- *Gearheads primarily reduce speed (RPM) to increase torque, allowing smaller motors to drive higher loads* #hairtransplanttool

## H

## I

**IEC 60601** - a series of international technical standards ensuring the basic safety and essential performance of medical electrical equipment. It applies to devices that have a direct physical connection to patients, transfer energy to/from them, or detect such transfers, including battery-operated or mains-powered equipment #QMS 

---

## O

**Onboard Sensors** - physical devices **integrated directly into a vehicle, machine, or structure** that detect or measure physical, biological, or chemical parameters, converting them into electronic signals for real-time monitoring, control, and data acquisition. In engineering, these sensors are critical for automation, safety, and performance tracking, allowing systems to understand their internal dynamics and external surroundings without relying on external infrastructure

**Peripheral Inputs** - auxiliary, external hardware devices or sensors that send data and instructions into a computer system or embedded controller
