# The Sound of Color

In the 1920s, a Russian painter named Alexander Laszlo built a *Farblichtklavier* — a "color-light piano" — that played colored light alongside sound, so each note had a corresponding hue flooding the room. He believed synesthesia wasn't a neurological quirk but a natural state humans had *trained themselves out of*. Some nights he performed to sold-out concert halls where the audience sat in shifting washes of violet and amber, hearing and seeing music at the same time. The instrument is lost. No recordings exist. Only photographs of the light.
