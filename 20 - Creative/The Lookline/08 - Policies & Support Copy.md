## Refund Policy

### Funds Timeline
- **Hotline:** Funds escrowed after stylist confirms. Released after session ends. Held if refund requested within the **48-hour dispute window**.
- **Scheduled:** Funds withdrawn after booking. Released after session ends. Held if refund requested within **48 hours post-session**, or if cancelled more than **24 hours before session**.

### Cancellation Rules

| Who cancels | When | Outcome |
|------------|------|---------|
| Client | More than 24h before session | Full refund |
| Client | Within 24h of session | No refund |
| Client | No-show | No refund |
| Stylist | Before session | Full refund |
| Stylist | During session (before delivering looks) | Full refund |

### Full Refund Triggers
- Stylist cancels before session starts
- Stylist ends session before delivering agreed number of looks
- Stylist relied mostly on purchased items (≥2 per outfit) instead of client's closet
- Stylist scored 0–3 on rubric (negligent service)

### Partial Refund Triggers
- Stylist delivered some looks but didn't meet requirements
- Stylist delivered but experience felt incomplete or rushed
- Client got what they booked but is unhappy — goodwill partial refund

### No Refund
- Client cancelled within 24h or no-showed
- Client ended session after agreed looks were delivered
- Session fully completed and client is satisfied

---

### Refund Decision Rubric (internal — admin use)

5 criteria, scored 0–2. Total out of 10.

| Criterion | 0 (Poor) | 1 (Partial) | 2 (Good) |
|-----------|----------|-------------|----------|
| Service Delivery | No looks OR fewer than half of agreed | Less than agreed, but majority provided | Full number delivered |
| Closet Integration | Majority rely on purchased items | Some closet use, heavy on purchases | Majority from closet |
| Responsiveness | Unresponsive, dismissive | Slow replies, minor tone lapses | Responsive, professional |
| Personalization | Outfit doesn't match archetype | 2+ items align with requirements | Clearly tailored |
| Client Experience | Session incomplete | One outfit but felt rushed | Smooth delivery |

| Score | Outcome |
|-------|---------|
| 0–3 | Full refund |
| 4–6 | Partial refund (30–50%) |
| 7–8 | Goodwill partial refund (20%) |
| 9–10 | No refund (admin discretion for extreme cases) |

### Public-Facing Refund Policy Copy
> *To be written — clear, direct, no legalese. Lookline voice.*

## Suspension System

### Warning
*Minor issues — account stays active, just flagged.*

**Triggers:** First missed session, multiple low-rated reviews, missing a session without cancelling, account unused 4+ months (stylist only).

**What happens:** In-app banner + email. Account stays active.

---

### Temporary Suspension
*~72 hours of limited access.*

**Triggers:** 3+ missed sessions, reported by 3+ users, not responding to 3 scheduled session requests, offering items/services during a session, accumulating 3 warnings.

**What happens:** Booking and real-time features disabled ~72 hours. Upcoming sessions cancelled. One appeal allowed.

---

### Indefinite Suspension
*Account disabled until appeal resolved.*

**Triggers:** Harassment or discrimination, sharing inappropriate/explicit content, fraud (chargebacks, fake accounts), multiple temporary suspensions.

**What happens:** Account access disabled. StyleCoin cashout blocked. One appeal; reviewed within 48–72 hours.

---

### Permanent Ban
*No path back.*

**Triggers:** 2+ indefinite suspensions, serious safety/fraud/legal concerns.

**What happens:** Account permanently closed. Re-registration blocked. No appeal eligible.

---

### Suspension Email Copy
> *To be written for each level — Warning, Temp, Indefinite, Permanent Ban*
> Voice note: warm but firm. User needs to understand what happened and what happens next.

## Unified Help Desk

### Support Email Flow
1. **Intake:** User contacts support → acknowledge with ticket number
2. **Progress update:** Respond / investigate
3. **Resolution:** Confirm outcome + feedback link
4. **Refund disputes:** Acknowledge with `Ticket #[XXXX]`. Note: *"Please respond within [X] days of our request to remain eligible for a refund."*

> *To be written: waitlist acknowledgement, FAQ ticket acknowledgement, dispute acknowledgement, resolution email*

## Notes
- The 48-hour dispute window is critical — must appear in session receipts AND refund FAQ copy
- The goodwill partial refund (score 7–8) is a brand trust moment — copy should feel generous, not bureaucratic
- Suspension email copy should never feel like an automated legal notice
