# IWD 2026 — Design System

> Visual language carries political weight. Every aesthetic decision either reinforces the message or undermines it.

---

## Palette — Suffragette Colors

| Role | Color | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Primary | Deep purple | Dominant — structure and weight |
| Secondary | Forest green | Contrast and tension |
| Base | White | Negative space, legibility |
| Accent | Yellow | Graphic only — use sparingly as a punch, not a theme |

**Hard rule:** No pink. No gradients. No soft shadows.

---

## Typography

| Use | Typeface | Notes |
|-----|---------|-------|
| Headline display | Bebas Neue / Druk Wide | Heavy, compressed — protest poster energy |
| Body / data | IBM Plex Mono | Technical, editorial, not decorative |
| Accent / texture | Courier Prime | Typewriter register — evokes print culture |

**Hard rule:** No Playfair Display. No decorative serifs. No fonts that signal "feminine" or "beginner."

---

## Texture & Treatment

- **Halftone screens** — applied to image areas and graphic elements
- **Duotone photo treatment** — images in palette colors, not full color
- **Grain overlay** — added at compositing layer
- **Hard offset shadows** — box shadows with no blur radius, stark contrast
- **Headline misregistration** — intentional slight offset on display type for print-register feel

**Hard rule:** No rounded corners (unless structurally necessary). No gentle shadows. No gradients.

## Anti-patterns (explicitly rejected)

- Pink in any shade
- Playfair Display or similar display serifs
- Gradients (linear or radial)
- Soft/blurred drop shadows
- Rounded corners as a decorative choice
- Anything that reads as "beginner" or trend-chasing
