# Sentido — Stickies and Highlights

> **Source:** *Sentido* book
> **Tags:** #books #philosophy #career #identity #systems-thinking
> **Related:** [[Design Concepts]] | [[ADP List - Careers in AI Panel]]

---

## On change and space

- The law of subtraction: you have to create space for change. If your life is too full, the opportunity goes to someone more available.
- The Goldilocks conundrum isn't about a perfect, unchanging middle — it's about being **agile** in pursuit of what feels right *right now*.
- **Intention = reflect + learn + adapt**

---

## On inclusion and missing voices

- Be conscious of the patterns you fall into and recognize when you're not making room at the table.
- *"Who is missing from this conversation, and how can we ensure they're being included?"*

---

## On radical candor and its limits

- When someone is being radically direct, they're assuming: you want to hear it, you'll perceive the intent as good, you won't be annoyed.
- *"I have certainly been in environments where I was encouraged to be radically candid but eventually punished for that same behavior."*

---

## On being fully expressed

- *"When we are swimming upstream, recognize the world has a problem with a fully expressed human — so make yourself even bigger."*
- Don't shrink to fit. Expand.

---

## On obligation and grief

> *"Do not be daunted by the enormity of the world's grief. Do justly now, love mercy now, walk humbly now. You are not obligated to complete the work, but neither are you free to abandon it."*

---

## On career and community

> *"View your career not just as a sequence of jobs or roles but as a carefully curated collection of intentional choices, each informed by and contributing to the community you cultivate around you."*

- *"When you get there, remember to keep the door open."*

---

## On love and making

- *"Perhaps it isn't 'love what you make,' but 'love how you make it.'"*

---

## On the journey

> *"There is no set path, no right or wrong, no one size fits all model. Now that I am here, I recognize there is no arrival; it is a continuous state of becoming. So then, the fight looks different now — instead, it's no longer a fight but a dance."*

---

## Connections to other notes

- "Law of subtraction" echoes the ADP List panel: if you outsource too much thinking to AI, you make space for someone else to own your relationships
- "Fully expressed human" maps to the red car question
- "Continuous state of becoming" is a useful frame for being early-career in AI/design
