## The Big Picture

- Models are evolving faster than applications — companies are scrambling to keep up
- Models are starting to function as operating systems
- 5 layers of AI: Chat → Applications → Models → Cloud → Chips
- Schools can't keep pace — accreditation cycles are too slow for AI's evolution

---

## Human in the Loop

- Started as a quality-check mechanism — low confidence in AI outputs
- The argument that "human in the loop becomes irrelevant at AGI" is wrong — it becomes *more* relevant, just differently
- **AI doesn't own the relationship. The human does.**
  - Sharing AI output without review = giving up authorship of your own relationships and outcomes
  - AI is a tool, not the solution
- "Building AI to fight the AI" — confidence scoring, real vs. fake detection
- Crucial to bring human judgment and rationality to real scenarios — AI only knows what it was trained on

> *"AGI is a marketing gimmick"*

---

## Breaking Into the Field

- Always have a project in your back pocket — one you can actually speak to
- Degrees carry less weight now: *"You're a CMU SWE grad. Great. What can you do with AI?"*
- **What hiring managers actually want:** curiosity, communication, problem-solving process, teamwork, how you interact with AI
- If there's no role listed, that doesn't mean there's no role
- Cold outreach with a genuine opinion or curiosity > generic messages

---

## What Skills Won't Disappear?

- "Lots of white cars, lots of black cars — a few red cars. What do you do to be a red car?"
- Critical thinking, curiosity, problem-solving used to be supplementary — now they're the main ticket
- Networking = crowdfunding your own product

---

## Portfolios & Resumes

- You are the product you're selling
- **Personality > copy-paste** — ATS doesn't catch soul
- **Blog / thought process documentation is crucial** — less about the work, more about how you think

---

## Meta-Prompting

- Ask *why*: "Why did you make this decision? Why do you think that?"
- Don't lose touch with human thought — Claude is trained on general knowledge, not *your* knowledge

> *"The deliverable isn't the value. The process is."*
> *"What can you do that AI can't? That's what matters most."*
> *"AI is like basic math. You need to know it like multiplication tables."*
