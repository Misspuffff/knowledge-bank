## Principles I keep coming back to

### Storytelling is design
Project timelines have narrative arcs. Teams are casts of characters. A kickoff meeting is Act 1. A good brief is a scene direction — it tells people what's happening without micromanaging how.

### Prompting is a design skill
Writing a good AI prompt and writing a good design brief are basically the same thing: define the problem, specify constraints, describe the desired outcome without over-prescribing the solution.

### The translator's edge
Being early-career and coming from a different background (writing, not traditional design school) means you have a perspective most people in the room don't have. Leverage it.

## Brand voice as a design skill (from The Lookline work)

**Copy is interface design.** Every word is a decision about where the user's attention goes.

- **Content drives layout.** Copy must be finalized before design locks.
- **Benefit-oriented, not literal.** Don't write what something *is*. Write what someone *gets*.
- **Register is everything.** Goal: sound like a real person saying it out loud, not a brand manager.
