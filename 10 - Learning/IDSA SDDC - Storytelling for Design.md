## Core Concepts

- Storytelling = controlling the narrative + communicating with clients
- People spend 30% of their time daydreaming *unless* engaged in a story
- Design for design's sake isn't enough — it needs a story behind it
- **Stories are 22x more memorable than facts**

> *"Stories are life with the dull bits cut out."* — Hitchcock

---

## Frameworks

### 5 / 5 / 50
- **5 seconds** to engage
- **5 minutes** for context
- **50 minutes** engaged

Use as a gut check for any presentation or case study structure.

### Engaging Work Story: What / So What / Now What
1. **What** — here's what happened / what I made
2. **So what** — here's why it matters
3. **Now what** — here's where it goes from here

### TED Story Structure
Hook → tension → insight → resolution. Works for portfolio presentations, client pitches, case studies.

## Explore vs. Explain

Two totally different modes. Know which one you're in.

| Mode | When to use | What it looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **Explore** | You don't know the answer yet | Finding patterns, building meaning |
| **Explain** | You know what you made and why | Communicating process clearly to others |

Most designers blur these — they explain when they should still be exploring, or explore in front of clients when they need to explain.

## Why Curiosity Is the Thing

- Curiosity keeps people listening
- BTU diagram: **confidence + curiosity** — you need both to tell a compelling story
- *"WHY?"* — why should people care? Understand the problem before you pitch the solution
